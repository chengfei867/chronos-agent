## Key findings (CONTEXT.md wall-candidates)

### F1 — **Retro-documentation is a valid round class** (R66 new)

Chronos has at least 3 features that shipped **before** their design doc + ADR: LangGraph adapter (Phase 1 early), CrewAI adapter scaffold (R52 before ADR-021 accepted), and now fork-tree viz (R34-A through R48-B before ADR-025). The pattern is: "feature grows incrementally through per-round progress docs; a retro design doc + ADR captures contract freeze at a minor-version boundary". This is a **legitimate non-anti-pattern** — agent-driven incremental shipping can outpace formal docs, and formalising at tag time is better than fabricating early-round ADRs. Adds to "In-place ADR promotion" (R57) as its sibling pattern: **retro design + contract-freeze ADR at minor-version boundary**.

### F2 — **Drift detection #2 success (R66 after R42-A)** 

R42-A was the first drift-detection success on chronos-agent (caught sandbox milestone after ADR-013 invalidated it). R66 is the second. Two successes in ~24 real-world rounds ≈ 8% of rounds catch drift, ROI ~3× time savings per hit. Worth keeping `roadmap-drift-detection` in the mandatory skill-scan list for future planning rounds.

### F3 — **CONTEXT.md's "先查 R37.5" hint was decisive** (R66 new)

The planning hint in CONTEXT §6 said *"查 R37.5 family-tree 做过什么 (first step 的 fork-tree work)"*. Without it, R66 would have likely opened `fork-tree-viz.md` skeleton and written ~400 lines speculating about what should exist before running a grep. The hint forced the audit-first order. **Takeaway**: planning-round TODOs in CONTEXT.md should always contain a "first check what exists" directive, not just "design this new thing". Adding this pattern to the CONTEXT.md §6 style guide.

### F4 — **ADR-018 + ADR-024 + ADR-025 form a triad** (R66 new)

- ADR-018 `compare` is `diff` — no new CLI subcommand (compare unified into diff)
- ADR-024 multi-pivot compare — Arc A slice 4 (compare auto-centroid)
- ADR-025 fork-tree-viz scope — Arc A item 2 (tree is tree, not compare)

The three together draw a clean boundary: **compare = row-alignment-of-N-runs**, **tree = DAG-of-forks-from-one-root**. Future Arc A additions slot into one or the other; no third category needed unless fork-DAG-structural-compare lands (deferred, Lee-2002 / POA territory).

### F5 — **"Planning round + audit-first" = more expensive than pure design, still single-slot** (R66 empirical)

R56/R57/R61 planning rounds: ~90-120 min, pure spec writing. R66 planning round: ~35-45 min audit + ~60-90 min writing 4 md artifacts. Still fits single-slot budget. Pre-budget for drift-detection rounds ≈ same as pure-design planning rounds — audit pays for itself by reducing artifact count and tightening scope.
